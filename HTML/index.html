Project Overview

Introduction

Analyzing the stock market has been a fascination for many market participants for many years. There are many ways to analyze the stock market â€“ including prices and fundamental factors.

Looking at the Fortune 100 or more specifically the wealthiest individuals in the world, it becomes apparent that business owners dominate the list. Our believe is that by purchasing a share of a company in the stock market, you are receiving a corresponding ownership interest in the business. As the business grows via sales and profitability the stock price follows this business growth.

This notion is embedded in our project, whereby we are trying to determine what drives stock prices. Some members of our team have experience in fundamental research of companies and that has become a starting point for predicting the stock price.

We wanted to determine whether certain fundamental factors could predict the stock price. By using multi-factor models, neural networks and machine learning we attempt to answer this question.

Our data set is quite robust, as we used the S&P 500 Index and its constituents (which is one of the most broad-based and quoted indexes in the world). We have data from 2005 to 2018, a combination of market prices/market values which we then compare to fundamental factors such as sales growth, operating earnings growth and net income growth.

We believe we have uncovered some interesting insights after analyzing our data looking at the relationship/correlation between fundamental factors to the market values.
